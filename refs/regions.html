    Regions

    Bali is made up of 8 regencies and the city of Denpasar. Below are some of the regions (excluding Bangli region -- because of lack of space)

    Jembrana

    The most remote regency, Jembarana has the least tourist presence. Most of the landscape consits of mountains, rice paddies and coastline. 3% of Bali's total land area is conserved here in the West Bali National Park.

    West Bali National Park

    Buleleng

    The northern most regency of Bali, it is home to the black sand beaches of Lovina and Pura Ulun Danu Bratan, an iconic lake-side temple built with a fusion of Hindu and Buddhist architecture.

    Pura Ulun Danu Bratan

    Karangasem

    Karangasem is home to Mount Agung -- an active volcano and the higest peak in Bali -- as well as Pura Besakih, a major Balinese tmeple and regarded as the largest and holiest temple of Balinese Hinduism.
    Pura Besakih

    Tabanan

    Tabanan is famous for Tanah Lot temple and the rice terraces of Jatiluwih -- UNESCO World Heritage site of Jatiluwih Rice Terraces. It is also Ariya's home regency!
    Tanah Lot TempleJatiluwih

    Bangli

    Bangli is the only landlocked regency in Bali. Popular activities include trekking Mount Batur and relaxing around Batur lake and the Kintamani highlands.
    Allergens: Coconut

    Badung

    This regency contains Bali's most heavily populated regions including: Kuta, Nusa Dua, Canggu, Uluwatu, Seminyak and Jimbaran. Most tourism occurs here.

    Allergens: Shrimp, Peanuts, Coconut

    Gianyar

    Gianyar is known as the art center of Bali and is home to the artsy, yet tranquil town of Ubud as well as the holy springs of Tirta Empul. In recent years, Ubud has begun attracting tourists more interested in arts, yoga and spirituality.

    Allergens: Coconut

    Klungkung

    Bali's smallest regency, 64.4% of Klungkung's land is made up of the offshore islands such as Nusa Penida and Nusa Ceningan. Klungkung palace can be found in this regency.
    Allergens: Soybeans

    And of course, can't forget about...
    Denpasar City

    The capitcal city of Bali, for many it will be the first point of entry given the airport is situated here. Home to popular sites such as Sanur beach.
    Allergens: Soybeans
